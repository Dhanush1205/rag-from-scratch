Cats have over 20 vocalizations, including the purr, meow, and hiss.
A group of cats is called a "clowder" and a group of kittens is called a "kindle".
Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping, which means a nine-year-old cat has been awake for only three years of its life.
A cat's purr vibrates at a frequency of 25 to 150 hertz, which is the same frequency that muscles and bones repair themselves.
Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
The technical term for a cat's hairball is a "bezoar".
A cat's nose print is unique, much like a human's fingerprint.
Cats can jump up to six times their length.
A cat has 32 muscles in each ear.
Cats can't taste sweetness.
A cat's brain is biologically more similar to a human brain than it is to a dog's.
Cats have a third eyelid called a "haw" that you rarely see.
Cats can run at speeds of up to 31 mph (49 km) over short distances.
A cat's heart beats nearly twice as fast as a human heart, at 110 to 140 beats per minute.
Cats have five toes on their front paws, but only four on their back paws.
A cat's field of view is about 200 degrees.
Cats have an extra organ that allows them to taste scents on the air.
A cat's whiskers are generally about the same width as its body.
Cats use their whiskers to determine if a space is too small to squeeze through.
The largest cat breed is the Ragdoll, males weigh 12 to 20 lbs.
